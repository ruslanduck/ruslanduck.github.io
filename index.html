No-Code & AI

Tools you can start
using today

You don't need a developer to start improving your operations. Here are tools non-technical founders can set up themselves. All free or have free tiers.

AI Meetings

Fathom

AI meeting recorder that auto-generates notes, action items, and summaries. Stop losing decisions in Zoom calls. Just focus on the conversation.

fathom.video — free plan available
CRM / Sales

Monday.com

Has ready-made CRM templates you can use instantly. Track leads, deals, follow-ups without building anything. Good enough until you outgrow it.

monday.com — free CRM templates
No-Code DB

Airtable

Like a spreadsheet that actually does things. Track inventory, clients, tasks, projects. Works like Excel but with real structure. Great for getting organized.

airtable.com — free for small teams
No-Code Automation

Make

Connect apps, automate workflows, and move data between systems — all visually, no code required. More powerful and flexible than basic automation tools.

make.com — free tier available
Knowledge Base

Notion

Document your processes, create team wikis, manage projects. If your onboarding is "shadow Dave for a month," Notion is step one to fixing that.

notion.so — free for personal use
Scheduling

Calendly

Stop the "when are you free?" email chains. Share a link, people book a time, it shows on your calendar. Saves hours of back-and-forth every week.

calendly.com — free basic plan
Async Video

Loom

Record quick video messages instead of writing long emails. Explain processes, give feedback, onboard new people. Faster and clearer than text.

loom.com — free plan available
Task Management

TickTick

Capture every task, organize by project, and build a trusted system using the GTD (Get Things Done) methodology. Stop losing track of what needs to happen next.

ticktick.com — free plan available

These tools are great starting points. When your business outgrows them and you need everything connected in one system, that's where we come in.
